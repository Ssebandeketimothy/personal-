TYPES OF FARMING THAT I PRACTICE

I practice Subsistence farming

subsistence farming, form of farming in which nearly all of the crops or livestock raised are used to maintain the farmer and the farmer's family, leaving little, if any, surplus for sale or trade. Preindustrial agricultural peoples throughout the world have traditionally practiced subsistence farming.Subsistence farming is described as family farming because it meets the needs of the farmer's family

I practice Commercial farming

Commercial farming is a farming in which an agricultural crop is grown for sale to and get profit. Commercial crop is typically purchased by parties separate from a farm.

I practice Mixed Farming

Mixed farming involves running a system of livestock and arable crops from the same farm and traditionally involves a broad range of crops and livestock being grown and raised, with the advantage of spreading the risk of any one crop failing in a given year.
